## Communication Framework

### Communication Philosophy

**Core Principles**
- **Transparency**: Open and honest communication
- **Timeliness**: Prompt notification of issues
- **Accuracy**: Precise and factual information
- **Consistency**: Uniform messaging across channels
- **Proactive**: Anticipate and prepare for communication needs
- **Respect**: Consider stakeholder needs and concerns

**Communication Goals**
- Maintain stakeholder confidence
- Minimize uncertainty and anxiety
- Provide clear guidance and expectations
- Demonstrate commitment to resolution
- Preserve business relationships
- Uphold brand reputation

### Stakeholder Classification

#### Stakeholder Categories
| Category | Examples | Interest Level | Communication Frequency |
|----------|----------|---------------|-------------------------|
| **Internal Leadership** | CEO, Board, Executives | High | Real-time during incidents |
| **Management Team** | VPs, Directors, Managers | High | Daily during incidents |
| **Technical Team** | IT, Security, Operations | High | Continuous during response |
| **Customers** | Enterprise clients, Partners | High | Immediate for major incidents |
| **Regulatory Bodies** | Government agencies, Auditors | Medium | As required by regulation |
| **Vendors & Partners** | Service providers, Integrators | Medium | Regular updates |
| **Employees** | All staff members | Medium | Internal updates |
| **General Public** | Media, Community | Low | As appropriate |

#### Communication Priorities
1. **P0 - Critical**: System-wide outage, data breach
2. **P1 - High**: Major service disruption
3. **P2 - Medium**: Partial service degradation
4. **P3 - Low**: Minor issues, maintenance

## Communication Procedures

### Incident Communication Matrix

#### Notification Tiers
| Tier | Trigger | Recipients | Timeline | Channel |
|------|--------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Tier 1 - Alert** | Incident detected | Response team | <15 minutes | Multiple |
| **Tier 2 - Update** | Ongoing response | Stakeholders | Every 30 minutes | Multiple |
| **Tier 3 - Resolution** | Incident resolved | All stakeholders | <1 hour | Multiple |
| **Tier 4 - Follow-up** | Post-incident | Stakeholders | Within 24 hours | Email |

#### Communication Channels
- **Primary**: Email, SMS, phone
- **Secondary**: Instant messaging, portal
- **Tertiary**: Social media, web updates
- **Emergency**: Broadcast notification systems

### Internal Communication

#### Leadership Communication
**Frequency**: Real-time during major incidents
**Content**: Strategic overview, resource allocation, decision points
**Responsibility**: Incident Commander

**Template - Leadership Briefing**:
```

- Status: [Current Status]
- Duration: [Time Elapsed]
- Business Impact: [Brief Impact Assessment]
- Next Steps: [Key Actions]
- Decision Points: [Required Decisions]

- Systems Affected: [List]
- Root Cause: [Known or Investigating]
- Recovery Progress: [Percentage/Details]

RESOURCE ALLOCATION:
- Teams Deployed: [List]
```

```template
**Current Status:**
- Impact: [Brief description of impact]
- Affected Services: [List of services]
- Resolution Time: [Estimated time]

**Our Actions:**

- [Action 2]
- [Action 3]

- You can contact support at [contact info]

The ERLMCP Team
```

**Template - Customer Resolution**:
```template
Subject: Service Resolved - [Service Name] - [Date/Time]

Dear [Customer],

We are pleased to inform you that the service issue affecting [service] has been resolved.

**Resolution Summary:**

- Resolution Time: [Total duration]

**Verification:**
- All systems operational
- Performance levels normal
- Data integrity verified

**Next Steps:**
- Continue normal operations
- Contact support if issues persist
- Monitor [service] for stability

We appreciate your patience during this time.

Sincerely,
The ERLMCP Team
```

### Communication Escalation

#### Escalation Path
```
Level 1: On-site communications team
Level 2: Crisis communications team
Level 3: Executive leadership

Level 5: Board of directors
```

#### Escalation Triggers
- Media inquiry escalation
- Social media sentiment shift
- Regulatory inquiry
- Customer escalation
- Executive demand
